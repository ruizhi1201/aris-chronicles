He sat with that number for a moment. Two hundred and eleven ideas. Some of them were half-sentences, the kind you jot at 2 AM when your brain mistakes exhaustion for creativity. Some were actual decks — forty, fifty slides, with market analysis and competitive landscapes and a slide titled Why Now that he'd filled in with varying degrees of conviction. One of them had a working prototype. He didn't let himself think about that one too long.

His phone buzzed. Elena, in Slack: Hey Kai — making sure you got the agenda. Also the video I sent Tuesday has some context for the Q2 section. He typed back: On it. Two words, which was technically true in the sense that he was aware the agenda existed.

A gate agent announced something incomprehensible over the intercom. A toddler three rows of seats away expressed a strong opinion about this. The man next to Kai smelled faintly of airport pretzels, which Kai now desperately wanted.
